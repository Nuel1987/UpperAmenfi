23RD ANNUAL REPORT

The Managing Director of ARB Apex Bank Ltd, Kasapreko Kwame Basanyin II, Omanhene of Wassa Amenfi Traditional Area, Odeneho Gyapong Ababio II, Omanhene of Sefwi Bekwai Traditional Area, Board Members of Sister Rural and Community Banks, Fellow Directors, Invited Guests, Nananom, Shareholders, Members of the Press,  distinguished ladies and gentlemen, it is my pleasure to welcome you to the 23rd  Annual General Meeting of Upper Amenfi Rural Bank Ltd which also happens to be the 25th anniversary of the bank.  I will present to you an overview of the bank’s activities for the year 2011.  Your directors will also present the financial performance of the bank for the year ended 31st December 2011.
The banking industry continued to be competitive in the year under review, with the proliferation of micro-finance institutions and other financial NGOs operating all over the country.  There is therefore the need to provide state of the art infrastructure to be able to meet the competition.  The move towards automation continues, with almost all branches of rural and community banks in the country fully computerized and net worked.  A customer of Upper Amenfi Rural Bank based at Sefwi Akontombra can now do business with the branch of the bank at Dunkwa or any other branch. Similarly, a customer of the bank can now transfer money and cash it at any rural bank almost instantly through Apex Link Money Transfer, now dubbed itrans.
Our bank is now linked to Western Union Money Transfer and Money- gram business through the power of computers.  All these call for increase in expenditure on equipment and other facilities.
In spite of these challenges, the bank continued to make progress in all areas of its operation.  I wish to begin with the profit for the year 2011.

PROFIT
The Bank declared profit before tax of GH¢342,037 in 2011 compared with GH¢251,089 in 2010.  This showed an increase of 36%.  The increase in profit came about as a result of an increase in the volume of operations.

ADVANCES
Advances increased from GH¢3,806,460 in 2010 to GH¢5,753,215 in 2011, showing an increase of 51%.  The Bank continued to grant bigger loans to both individuals and groups, and this accounted for the increase in advances.
However, there is still a high rate of default, and this is preventing the bank from enjoying the full benefits from the increase in loans.

DEPOSIT
Total deposits increased from GH¢12,636,062 in 2010 to GH¢17,549,548 in 2011, showing an increase of 39%.  Shareholders are again encouraged to patronize the bank to help move the bank forward.

INVESTMENT
The Bank’s short term investment increased from GH¢4,411,275 in 2010 to GH¢7,078,780 in 2011, this shows an increase of 60% over the previous year’s figure.  The Bank also invested GH¢143,673 in the shares of the ARB Apex Bank Ltd, as against GH¢108,858 in 2010.

TOTAL ASSETS
The Total Assets of the bank increased from GH¢15,836,003 in 2010 to GH¢19,629,874 in 2011, recording an increase of 24%.

This increase is due to the construction works that took place at the Head Office and Sefwi Bekwai, and the acquisition of other assets, such as generators and other equipments.

SHARE HOLDERS FUNDS
Shareholders funds increased from GH¢1,011,401 in 2010 to GH¢1,361,894 in 2011, showing an increase of 35%.

DIVIDEND
The Directors recommend the payment of GH¢0.03 per share.  With the total number of shares at 1,976,600, total dividends payment is projected to be GH¢59,298.  This is an indication of better returns on shareholders investment, and shareholders are invited to purchase more shares to help raise the stated capital of the bank.

EDUCATION
The bank continues to award scholarships to needy but brilliant shareholders’ wards, and every year, five students are awarded.
Advertisement will soon be made for interviews and examination early next year to select candidates for the awards.  The bank has also sponsored a number of its staff to pursue programs in various Universities.  As at today two Management staff have completed their Masters degree programs with over ten members of staff having completed first degree programs and a similar number with diploma certificates.  It is our hope that all these workers would remain in the bank to contribute their quota to the development of the bank.

SEFWI BEKWAI BUILDING PROJECT
Fellow shareholders, the Sefwi Bekwai Office building which started a little over a year ago is about 95% completed.  It is expected to be ready for use at the beginning of next year.  Movement to the new office building will help ease the congestion at the current office.

GHANA CLUB 100 AWARDS (2011 EDITION)
 Ladies and Gentlemen the bank again made it to the Ghana Club 100 for the third time in 2011 rankings, moving from 56th position in 2009 to the 42nd position in 2010 and to the 36th position in 2011.
We once again dedicate this Award to the honour of the board, Shareholders, Management and Staff, and all stakeholders and hope that with the support of everybody, we can move further up the ladder in 2012.

RETIREMENT
The following Directors are due to retire by rotation this year, and they are eligible for re-election.  They have expressed the desire to contest for another term, and we count on you to give them your mandate so that they bring their experience to bear on the progress of the bank. They are:  1. Mr. Anthony Donkor and
2. Kwame Amankwaah.

CONCLUTION
I am very grateful to the Board, Management and Staff, Shareholders and our cherished customers for your continued support encouragement and commitment to the progress of the Bank.
We pray that you will continue to support us so that together we can  move the bank foreword.
Thank you and may God bless us all.
