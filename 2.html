HISTORY

Upper Amenfi Rural Bank Ltd started as an Agency of Amefiman Rural Bank Ltd in 1982.  The aim then was to mobilize shares and deposits for the headquarters at Wassa Akropong.
In August, 1987, the founding fathers of this bank decided to break away from Amenfiman Rural Bank Ltd to form a new one at Ankwawso.  The main objective was to mobilize deposit and cultivate the habbit of savings in the people of the catchment area.  Ankwawso was chosen as the head quarters of the bank due to it strategic position as the central point for people from the Denkyira, Sefwi  and Wassa Areas.
Another objective of the bank was also to assist farmers to clear their Akuafo Cheques which had been introduced in the country for the first time.

FOUNDING FATHERS
The main architects of the bank were Nana Kwadwo Nuamah II,  Chief of Wassa Ankaasie, Lawyer Ohene Kofi Duah, both of blessed memory,  Nana Kofi Boakye I, Chief of Ankwawso.

PROMOTERS
The key promoters of the bank who assisted to mobilize shares for the start of the bank were the Late Mr. Amos Tano, Mr. James Acquah, Nana Amano, Mr. Kwabena Sonful, and Opanin Kwaku Moses.
These people travelled the length and breadth of the bank’s catchment areas mostly on foot to convince people to purchase shares.

PERFORMANCE
The Bank performed very well in the initial stages of its establishment, between 1990 to 1995.  This was due to the boom in the cocoa industry and the emphases placed on Akuafo Cheques purchases.
During this period, the use of Akuafo Cheques in the purchase of cocoa beans
became mandatory for all cocoa buying companies, and since the bank  became the sole agent in this exercise,  the bank earned a lot of income  from Akuafo Cheques Commission.  Most of the Agencies of the bank were located in farming communities such as Nkronua, Ntom, Mpatasie, Adjakaa Manso and Agona Amenfi .
The bank was then rated among the satisfactory banks in the country.
However, in the mid- 1990’s the bank started to face some set backs.  This was due partly to mismanagement and partly to the collapse the Akuafo cheques system.  With the liberalization of the Cocoa Marketing System, and the proliferation of Cocoa buying companies, many of those companies started cash purchases of cocoa instead of resorting to the use of Akuafo  cheques.  And because the management of the bank failed to undertake any serious commercial  banking,  the fortunes of the bank started to decline.
During this period, the bank changed four managers within a period of 4 years (1994 – 1998).

From 1998, the Management of the bank decided to open up and entered into  serious deposit mobilization through susu and micro-finance operations.

Credit officers were appointed to handle the micro finance  business and this replaced  Akuafo Cheque commission as a major source of income for the bank.  A loan of GH¢5,000 (old fifty Million Cedis) granted to the bank by the ARB/Danida Project enabled the bank to  organise micro –finance  groups first in Sefwi Humjibre and later at Sefwi Bekwai.  An Agency was established at Sefwi Bekwai in 2002 and Sefwi Dwenase in 2005.
With these two agencies, the bank  was able to venture into commercial banking and further  expanded its micro-finance operation.
As at today, the bank has 8 agencies located at Ankwawso, Diaso, Dunkwa, Sefwi Bekwai, Sefwi Dwenase, Sefwi Wiawso (Watico Campus) Sefwi Akontombra and  Agona Amenfi.

. With a modest profit ofGH¢11,244 in 2002 the bank has been able to attain a profit of ¢342,000 in 2011, and succeeded in puting up its head office building and an ultra modern office at Sefwi Bekwai. The office building at Agona Amenfi and Sefwi Wiawso (Watico) are also owned by the bank.
Upper Amenfi Rural bank now ranks among the satisfactory rural banks in Ghana, and has been a member of Ghana Club 100 for three consecutive years.
Upper Amenfi Rural Bank is now 25 years old and it is the vision of the Board and Management to be among the leading Rural Banks in the country.
This vision is being pursued to the letter and the future of the bank looks bright.

The bank can become among the 5 leading rural banks in the country in the very near future with the support and goodwill of all stakeholders
